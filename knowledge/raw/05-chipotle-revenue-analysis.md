## Revenue Drivers

### Digital & Delivery
- Digital orders represent 37.4% of total revenue (FY 2023)
- Chipotlane locations show higher average unit volumes than traditional units
- Third-party delivery partnerships (DoorDash, Uber Eats) provide incremental reach

### Pricing Power
- Chipotle has taken multiple menu price increases since 2021 to offset labor and food cost inflation
- Same-store sales growth driven by combination of transaction growth and check average increases

### New Unit Openings
- Company targets 285–315 new restaurant openings per year
- New restaurants predominantly include Chipotlane (drive-thru pickup window)
- International expansion (Middle East, Asia) adds incremental revenue streams

## Growth Slowdown Analysis

The deceleration from 14.61% (FY 2024) to 5.41% (FY 2025) reflects:
1. Tougher year-over-year comparisons after exceptional post-pandemic recovery
2. Consumer spending pressure in lower-income demographics
3. Pricing fatigue after several rounds of menu price increases
4. CEO transition uncertainty (Brian Niccol → Scott Boatwright, August 2024)
