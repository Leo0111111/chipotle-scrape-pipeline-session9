# Chipotle Mexican Grill: Comprehensive Overview

## Company History

Chipotle Mexican Grill was founded on July 13, 1993, by Steve Ells in Denver, Colorado. Ells opened the first location in a former Dolly Madison Ice Cream store at 1644 East Evans Avenue near the University of Denver campus, using an $85,000 loan from his father.

**Early Growth**: The original restaurant exceeded expectations dramatically. While projections indicated the need to sell 107 burritos daily to break even, the store was "selling over 1,000 burritos a day" within one month. The second location opened in 1995 using cash flow from the original restaurant.

**McDonald's Investment**: In 1998, McDonald's became a major investor when Chipotle had only 16 locations (all in Colorado). This partnership accelerated expansion significantly—by 2005, the company had grown to over 500 locations. McDonald's invested approximately $360 million but divested completely in October 2006, ultimately receiving $1.5 billion in return.

**Public Company Status**: Chipotle went public on January 26, 2006. The stock rose exactly 100% on its first day, marking "the best U.S.-based IPO in six years, and the second-best IPO for a restaurant after Boston Market."

**International Expansion**: The first location outside the United States opened in Toronto in August 2008, followed by European locations in London (2010), Paris (2012), and Frankfurt (2013). As of 2025, Chipotle operates in eight countries with 4,042 locations.

## Business Model

**Company-Owned Operations**: All Chipotle restaurants are company-owned rather than franchised, ensuring consistent quality and brand control.

**Menu Strategy**: The company maintains a focused menu consisting of five items: burritos, bowls, tacos, quesadillas, and salads. Protein options include chicken, carnitas, barbacoa, steak, and plant-based alternatives. Ells emphasized that "keeping the menu focused" ensures superior execution.

**Food Preparation**: Most food is prepared fresh in each location daily. Exceptions include beans and carnitas, which are centrally prepared in Chicago. Restaurants maintain no freezers, microwave ovens, or can openers.

**Chipotlane Initiative**: In 2018, Chipotle introduced "Chipotlane" drive-thru windows dedicated to mobile order pickup. As of 2023, over 500 locations feature this service.

## Food Safety and Quality

Chipotle has experienced multiple food-borne illness outbreaks, particularly during 2015:

- **July 2015**: E. coli outbreak affecting five people in Seattle
- **August 2015**: Norovirus outbreak affecting 200+ people in Simi Valley; Salmonella outbreak affecting 64 people across Minnesota locations
- **October 2015**: E. coli (STEC O26) outbreak affecting 55 people across 11 states
- **July 2018**: Clostridium perfringens outbreak affecting over 700 people in Powell, Ohio

In response to 2015 outbreaks, the company closed all locations on February 8, 2016, for an all-staff food safety meeting and implemented enhanced protocols including more frequent handwashing and produce sanitation procedures.

## Sourcing Philosophy

**Food with Integrity**: Launched in 2001, this mission statement emphasizes naturally raised meat, organic produce, and hormone-free dairy. The company sources from open-range pork suppliers and has committed to antibiotic-free beef.

**Supply Chain Standards**: In October 2012, Chipotle signed an agreement with the Coalition of Immokalee Workers to improve farmworker conditions. The company also eliminated genetically modified organisms (GMOs) from its menu by 2015.

## Technology and Innovation

**Mobile Ordering**: Launched iPhone app in 2009; Android app followed in 2013. Online ordering generates significant revenue—37.4% in 2023.

**Automation**: In September 2024, Chipotle partnered with Vebu on "Autocado," an automated avocado processing machine capable of peeling and cutting avocados in under 30 seconds.

**Drone Delivery**: In August 2025, Chipotle announced partnerships with Zipline to begin drone delivery service in the Dallas area.

## Expansion Strategy

**Domestic Goals**: As of 2023, Chipotle announced plans to hire 15,000 workers and expand to 7,000 locations, effectively doubling its footprint from the approximately 3,700 at that time.

**International Partnerships**: In July 2023, Chipotle partnered with Kuwait-based Alshaya Group in its first franchise deal, expanding into the Middle East. Additionally, in September 2025, the company signed a joint venture with SPC Group to enter Eastern Asian markets, with restaurants opening in South Korea and Singapore in 2026.
